Cervical cancer remains one of the most common cancers among women, especially in low-resource settings where access to regular screening is limited.

As part of our thesis, we developed a predictive system designed to assess cervical cancer risk using machine learning techniques.

## 🧠 The Idea

The goal of the system is to provide a non-invasive and accessible way to help identify individuals who may be at risk. This allows healthcare providers, such as midwives, to make informed decisions and encourage early screening like Pap smears.

## ⚙️ Technologies Used

- XGBoost for predictive modeling  
- Optuna for hyperparameter optimization  
- SHAP for model interpretability  

These tools helped us not only build an accurate model but also understand how different factors contribute to the predictions.

## 📊 Key Features

- Risk assessment based on patient data  
- Interpretable results using SHAP values  
- User-friendly interface for healthcare workers  

## 🌍 Impact

The system is designed specifically for low-resource settings, where access to advanced medical tools may be limited. By providing an early risk assessment, it can help improve awareness and encourage preventive healthcare.

- Machine learning and data analysis  
- System design and development  
- Applying technology to real-world problems  

It also showed me how technology can play a role in improving healthcare accessibility.
